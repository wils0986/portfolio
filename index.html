The Atlas of Canada Website Redesign

The Atlas of Canada website was redesigned to improve information architecture and user experience, informed by user research, competitive analysis, stakeholder feedback, and design and content reviews. A custom banner showcasing the Atlas's evolution from 1906 to today supported the launch as part of a strategic communications plan that included social media, newsletter features, webpage updates, cross-linking on Government of Canada sites, and a roll-up poster for events.

GEO.ca Website Redesign

Geo.ca was redesigned with a focus on enhancing user experience through improved information architecture, accessibility, and mobile-first design. A major enhancement was the overhaul of the search function, replacing basic keyword search with a more intuitive semantic search to improve content discoverability. The redesign process included conducting user research, stakeholder engagement, and the development of interactive prototypes for A/B testing.

GEO.ca Permafrost Community

Geo.ca's first dedicated Community Space is a central hub for permafrost information in Canada, providing data, tools, and resources from provinces and territories to support research and decision-making. Content curation included in-depth research of each region's permafrost conditions and monitoring methods, consultation with provincial and territorial stakeholders such as the Canadian Council on Geomatics Task Team, and obtaining photos from each region along with permission to use and attribute them.

GEO.ca GeoAI Banner

A custom banner showcasing GeoAI's Deep Learning extraction of buildings, hydrography, forests, and roads from aerial or satellite imagery was developed as part of a strategic communications plan. This plan also included social media posts, a feature in the quarterly branch newsletter, a spotlight at the 2025 GC Data Conference, and ongoing content updates to the GeoAI webpage.

Canada's Collaborative Geospatial Data Strategy Banner

A custom banner was created as part of a strategic communications plan to support the Collaborative Geospatial Data Strategy for Canada callout, which sought input from Canadians on the future of accessing, using, and sharing location data. The plan also included a UX review of the webpage, social media posts, a feature in a feature in the GC Data Community Newsletter, and a spotlight at the 2025 GC Data Conference.
